## Challenge Patterns

_Recurring categories of weak assumptions and plan vulnerabilities._

### Optimistic Market Assumptions
- **Symptoms**: Sales timelines based on ideal-case buyer behavior; no allowance for procurement/IT/legal gatekeeping in enterprise targets
- **Challenge approach**: Ask: "What is the median sales cycle for this buyer type? Who else must approve this purchase?"
- **Historical pattern**: B2B SaaS startups routinely underestimate enterprise sales cycles by 2-4x

### Survivorship Bias in Analogies
- **Symptoms**: "Company X did Y and succeeded" used as proof that Y works
- **Challenge approach**: Ask: "How many companies tried Y and failed? Are the conditions the same?"

### Consensus-Without-Dissent
- **Symptoms**: No one in the room argued against the decision; decision reached in under 5 minutes; everyone uses the word "obvious"
- **Challenge approach**: Explicitly construct the strongest opposing case; ask "what would have to be true for this to fail?"

### Single-Point-of-Success Plans
- **Symptoms**: The plan works only if one key assumption holds (a specific partnership, a specific regulatory outcome, a specific technology behaving as expected)
- **Challenge approach**: Pre-mortem focused on that single dependency; ask for a Plan B

### Scope Optimism
- **Symptoms**: MVP includes features whose necessity is assumed rather than validated; "users will definitely want this"
- **Challenge approach**: Ask: "Which of these features has been validated by a real user? What is the minimum that tests the core hypothesis?"
